Frequently asked questions

How many calories are in a gram of protein, carbs, and fat, and why 4/4/9?

Protein and carbohydrates each provide about 4 kilocalories per gram, while fat provides about 9 kilocalories per gram — these are the classic Atwater factors used on nutrition labels worldwide. That's why a diet split described in percentages, like 30% protein / 40% carbs / 30% fat, doesn't translate into equal grams: on a 2,000-kcal day, 30% protein is 600 kcal ÷ 4 = 150 g, but 30% fat is 600 kcal ÷ 9 = only 67 g. This macro calculator does that division automatically for every macro, so you get grams you can weigh on a food scale instead of percentages you'd have to convert yourself.

How is my daily calorie target calculated if I don't know it?

Switch to the 'Calculate my calories' tab and enter your sex, age, height, and weight. The tool first estimates your basal metabolic rate (BMR) with the Mifflin-St Jeor equation — 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age, +5 for men or −161 for women — then multiplies it by an activity factor from 1.2 (sedentary) to 1.9 (extra active) to get your total daily energy expenditure. Finally it applies your goal: about 80% of that number for weight loss, 100% to maintain, or roughly 110% for a lean muscle-gain surplus. The result feeds straight into the macro split below, so you never have to copy numbers between calculators.

Which diet split should I choose — balanced, low-carb, high-protein, keto, or custom?

Balanced (30% protein / 40% carbs / 30% fat) suits most general fitness and weight-management goals and is easy to sustain long-term. Low-carb (40/20/40) and keto (25/5/70) cut carbohydrates for people managing blood sugar or following a ketogenic approach, with keto going low enough to typically trigger ketosis. High-protein (40/40/20) favors muscle building or preservation during a calorie deficit, since protein has the highest satiety and helps protect lean mass. If none of the presets fit, choose Custom and drag the three sliders — they always rebalance to add up to 100%, so you can dial in exactly the ratio your coach or dietitian recommends.

What does IIFYM mean, and does this calculator support flexible dieting?

IIFYM stands for 'if it fits your macros' — a flexible-dieting approach where you can eat any food, as long as its protein, carbs, and fat add up to your daily targets. The Custom split mode is built for exactly this: set your own protein, carb, and fat percentages, for example based on a coach's recommendation, and this macro calculator turns them into grams you can track in any food-logging app. Keep in mind this tool computes calorie and macro targets only — it doesn't account for fiber, micronutrients, or food quality, so IIFYM works best alongside otherwise reasonably nutritious eating rather than as a substitute for it.

Is this medical advice, and does my data leave my browser?

No to both. Every calculation — the BMR estimate, the calorie target, and the macro split — runs entirely in your browser using the numbers you type; nothing is uploaded, logged in, or sent to any server, and your last inputs are saved only in your browser's local storage for next time. This calculator provides educational, formula-based estimates for general fitness and nutrition planning, not personalized medical, dietary, or clinical advice — if you have a health condition, are pregnant, or want a plan tailored to you, please consult a doctor or registered dietitian.
