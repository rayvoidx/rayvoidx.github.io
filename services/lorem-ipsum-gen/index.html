Frequently asked questions

What is Lorem Ipsum, and why do designers use it as placeholder text?

Lorem Ipsum is scrambled, semi-random Latin text derived from Cicero's 1st-century BC essay "de Finibus Bonorum et Malorum". Designers and developers have used variations of it since the 1500s because it has a natural-looking distribution of letters and word lengths, unlike "Content here, content here" placeholders, so a reader's eye treats it like normal text and doesn't get distracted by a familiar language. Because the words are essentially meaningless, it also doesn't bias a client's opinion of the layout with real marketing copy. This generator produces fresh, grammatically loose Latin-style passages built from that same classic word bank.

Is the text truly random, or does it repeat the same passage every time?

Each click reshuffles a seeded pseudo-random sequence over the classic Lorem Ipsum vocabulary, so consecutive generations read differently even with identical settings — no two passages are the same. Turning on "Start with the classic opening" keeps the familiar first sentence, "Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipiscing elit," fixed at the top while everything after it is freshly generated. Turn that option off for a fully randomized passage from the first word.

How do I get Lorem Ipsum wrapped in HTML <p> tags for a website mockup?

Check "Wrap paragraphs in HTML <p> tags" before generating: each paragraph is then wrapped in its own <p>…</p> tag so you can paste the result straight into an HTML template, CMS, or component library without adding markup by hand. In sentence or word mode the whole block is wrapped in a single <p> tag. Leave the option unchecked to get plain text with blank lines between paragraphs instead, ready for a plain-text field or a word processor.

What's the difference between generating by paragraphs, sentences, or words?

Paragraphs mode builds full multi-sentence paragraphs (roughly 3–6 sentences each) and is the best fit for filling in body copy or article mockups. Sentences mode produces a single run of complete sentences, handy for captions, alt text, or a short blurb. Words mode outputs a plain stream of individual words with no paragraph breaks, useful for testing how a UI truncates or wraps a fixed word count. Each mode has its own allowed range shown next to the count field, and out-of-range values are automatically clamped.

Is my generated text saved or sent anywhere?

No. Every passage is generated entirely inside your browser using a local word bank — nothing is uploaded, and no request is sent to any server. Your last-used settings (mode, count, and the two toggles) are saved only in your browser's local storage so the tool remembers your preference next time, and you can clear that at any time by clearing your browser data.
