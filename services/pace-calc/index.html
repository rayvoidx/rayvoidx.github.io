How do I calculate my running pace from a race time?

Pace is your time divided by distance. Formula: pace per km = total time ÷ distance in km (or per mile = total time ÷ distance in miles). For example, if you run a 5K (5 km) in 25:00, your pace is 25:00 ÷ 5 = 5:00 per km — pick “Find Pace”, enter 5 km and 25:00, and this calculator does the division for you, showing the result in both min/km and min/mi.

How do I convert min/km to min/mile pace?

Multiply your pace in minutes per kilometer by 1.60934 (the number of kilometers in a mile) to get minutes per mile — a 5:00/km pace becomes about 5:00 × 1.60934 ≈ 8:03/mi. Going the other way, divide a min/mile pace by 1.60934 to get min/km. This calculator converts automatically: enter your pace in either unit and the result panel always shows both min/km and min/mi side by side.

Can this calculator predict my race finish time?

Yes — switch to “Find Time”, enter your goal race distance (use the 5K, 10K, half marathon, or marathon presets, or type a custom distance), and enter the pace you know you can hold from a recent race or training run. The calculator multiplies your pace by the distance to give a projected finish time, plus a full split table showing your cumulative time at every kilometer or mile along the way.

What time formats does the time field accept?

You can type hours:minutes:seconds (1:32:15), just minutes:seconds (45:00), or a plain number of minutes (45). The pace field works the same way for minutes:seconds — 5:30 means 5 minutes 30 seconds. The parser is tolerant of missing leading zeros and decimal seconds, so “1:5:9” and “1:05:09.5” both work.

Is my pace and distance data sent to a server?

No. Every calculation — distance, time, pace, speed, and the split table — runs entirely in your browser. Nothing is uploaded, there's no sign-up, and your last entries are only saved in your browser's local storage so the form remembers them next time. This isn't training advice from a coach; it's arithmetic based on the numbers you enter.
