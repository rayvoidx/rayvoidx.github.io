How do you calculate my next 6 period dates?

This period calculator adds your average cycle length to the first day of your last period, then repeats that six times to project the start of your next 6 periods. Each period's end date is the start plus your period length minus one day. For example, a last period starting January 1 with a 28-day cycle and a 5-day period gives a next period from January 29 to February 2, then February 26 to March 2, and so on. The further out a projection is, the more small cycle variations can shift the real date.

How is my ovulation day and fertile window estimated?

Each cycle's ovulation day is estimated as that cycle's period start minus 14 days, using the standard luteal-phase method — the time from ovulation to the next period is the most stable part of the menstrual cycle at about 14 days, while the days before ovulation vary more. The fertile window then runs from 5 days before ovulation to 1 day after, a 7-day span, because sperm can survive a few days in fertile cervical mucus while the egg lives only about a day after release.

Can I use this period calculator instead of birth control?

No. This tool estimates dates from averages, and real cycles shift with stress, illness, travel, sleep and many other factors, so calendar-based predictions are not a reliable form of contraception — with typical use, calendar or rhythm methods fail for roughly 12–24 out of every 100 women in a year. Use it for planning and awareness only, and ask a doctor about a proven contraceptive method if you need one.

What if my cycle length or period length isn't always the same?

Enter the average of your last 3–6 cycles and treat every date below as a rough estimate rather than a guarantee. If your cycle length swings by more than a few days, falls outside 21–45 days, or your period length falls outside 2–10 days, these projections become less reliable — an ovulation test kit (LH strips), basal body temperature tracking, or a doctor's advice will serve you better than the calendar alone.

Is my period data stored or sent anywhere?

No. Every date is calculated locally in your browser with JavaScript — your last period date, cycle length and period length are saved only in this browser's local storage so the form is ready next time, and "Clear saved data" removes them immediately. Nothing is uploaded, there's no account, and no one else can see what you enter.
